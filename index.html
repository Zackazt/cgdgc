History Of Disc Golf Growth In Columbus

In 2009 Jody Mitchell, Jayson Camper and Nick Wrenn started the process with the city to design build a disc golf course at Flatrock. In September 2010 the course was completed and the sport has grown in Columbus ever since. Flat Rock Park is an 18 hole course with 3 pin placements for each hole and pars ranging from 3 to 5. The course is playable in all seasons and has a range of holes that challenge players with elevation change, wooded fairways, rock formations, Mandos, etc. There are now 4 other courses in the area; Dinglewood park (nice wooded 9 hole, par 3), The River Course (18 hole course on the Phenix city riverbank) Aflac Course (private course for employees with 9 holes) and Russ Pond (9 hole par 3 on Ft Benning).

Compete For Tags

Every year the club sells Bag Tags. By purchasing the Tag you become an official member of the Columbus GA Disc Golf Club for that year. The tags are meant to be attached to player's bags and are played for throughout the year. They can be played for in casual play, organized events, tournaments, etc. Tag play is not limited to Flat Rock. Tags can be played for at any course whether in Columbus or not. Most tag play happens in rounds with a group of players but members may also issue a challenge to another player with a lower tag and the challenged player shoulbased on score, lowest tag goes to the lowest score. In case of a tie the person with the lowest tag coming into the round takes the lowest tag.
